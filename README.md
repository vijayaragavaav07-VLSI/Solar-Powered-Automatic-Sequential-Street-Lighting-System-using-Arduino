Introduction

  The Solar Street Light System is an eco-friendly lighting solution that uses solar energy to power street lights. The system collects sunlight through a solar panel during the daytime, stores the energy in a rechargeable battery, and automatically powers the LED light during nighttime. This reduces electricity consumption and promotes the use of renewable energy.

Objective

    To utilize solar energy for street lighting.
    To reduce dependence on conventional electricity.
    To provide automatic lighting during nighttime.
    To create an energy-efficient and environmentally friendly solution.
    
Components Used

    Solar Panel
    Rechargeable Battery
    LED Street Light
    Charge Controller
    LDR (Light Dependent Resistor) Sensor
    Connecting Wires
    Pole/Supporting Structure
    
Working Principle

  During the daytime, the solar panel absorbs sunlight and converts it into electrical energy.
  The generated energy is stored in the rechargeable battery through the charge controller.
  The LDR sensor continuously monitors the surrounding light intensity.
  When it becomes dark, the LDR detects low light levels and automatically switches ON the LED street light.
  During sunrise, the LDR senses daylight and automatically switches OFF the LED light while the battery begins charging again.
  
Advantages

  Uses renewable solar energy.
  Reduces electricity costs.
  Automatic operation without manual control.
  Low maintenance requirements.
  Environmentally friendly and pollution-free.
  
Applications

  Streets and highways
  Parks and gardens
  College and school campuses
  Rural and remote areas
  Parking lots and public spaces
  
Conclusion

The Solar Street Light System developed by Silicon Flames Team demonstrates an effective use of renewable energy for public lighting. The project provides a sustainable, cost-effective, and environmentally friendly solution that can help reduce energy consumption and support green technology initiatives.
